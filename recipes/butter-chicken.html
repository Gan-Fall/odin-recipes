Butter Chicken

Description

Butter Chicken is a curry recipe very similar to Chicken Tikka Masala but instead of tomato paste, it uses a butter and spiced tomato gravy which the dish is cooked in.

If you're not making the gravy yourself, it is a very easy and delicious recipe that is very creamy and tender. Can't go wrong with this one!

Ingredients

  • Butter chicken sauce.
  • A stick of butter.
  • Chicken meat.
  • Your choice of rice or naan bread. Or both.
  • Your favorite assortment of spices or vegetables that you might want to add. Get creative! Here are some suggestions:
    • Carrots
    • Garlic
    • Rosemary
    • Parsley

Steps

  1. Cut your chicken meat into small (around 1 inch) cube-like slices.
  2. Start heating up your frying pan in a medium-high fire.
  3. Cut a roughly 2 or 3 inch cube from the stick of butter and let it melt in the frying pan. Make sure to rotate the pan to make the butter melt evenly
    throughout the frying pan. By the end you want to have a nice little puddle that will blend and simmer with the chicken and the sauce later.
  4. Once the temperature in the pan has picked up enough and the butter is sizzling, its time to drop in the chicken meat.
  5. Wait around 3 minutes while moving the meat around for the meat to seal and whiten all over.
  6. Once the meat is properly sealed, drop in the butter chicken sauce followed by the spices.
  7. Lower the fire to a low and place the lid on the frying pan
  8. After letting the meat simmer for about another 30 minutes, your chicken is ready. Serve it with your choice of side and enjoy!

Tips

  • If you're using store bought sauce, you should have used about half the bottle. Leaving you with enough for another round. If you used up the second half, you can
    rinse the bottle with water to get the last bits of sauce and drop it in as well into the pan. Don't use too much water so you don't get splashed.
  • You can save a lot of money and be less wasteful by buying a whole chicken and cutting out the pieces of meat yourself, but if you don't feel comfortable wih
    filleting the chicken yourself (or don't have time) , you can simply buy whole chicken breasts and cut them into cubes.
